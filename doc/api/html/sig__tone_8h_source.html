29 /*! \page sig_tone_page The 2280/2400/2600Hz signalling tone processor
30 \section sig_tone_sec_1 What does it do?
31 The signalling tone processor handles the 2280Hz, 2400Hz and 2600Hz tones, used
32 in many analogue signalling procotols, and digital ones derived from them.
33 
34 \section sig_tone_sec_2 How does it work?
35 Most single and two voice frequency signalling systems share many features, as these
36 features have developed in similar ways over time, to address the limitations of
37 early tone signalling systems.
38 
39 The usual practice is to start the generation of tone at a high energy level, so a
40 strong signal is available at the receiver, for crisp tone detection. If the tone
41 remains on for a significant period, the energy level is reduced, to minimise crosstalk.
42 During the signalling transitions, only the tone is sent through the channel, and the media
43 signal is suppressed. This means the signalling receiver has a very clean signal to work with,
44 allowing for crisp detection of the signalling tone. However, when the signalling tone is on
45 for extended periods, there may be supervisory information in the media signal, such as voice
46 announcements. To allow these to pass through the system, the signalling tone is mixed with
47 the media signal. It is the job of the signalling receiver to separate the signalling tone
48 and the media. The necessary filtering may degrade the quality of the voice signal, but at
49 least supervisory information may be heard.
50 */

66 /* Mode control and report bits for transmit and receive */
67 enum
68 {
69  /*! Signalling tone 1 is present */
70  SIG_TONE_1_PRESENT = 0x001,
71  /*! Signalling tone 1 has changed state (ignored when setting tx mode) */
72  SIG_TONE_1_CHANGE = 0x002,
73  /*! Signalling tone 2 is present */
74  SIG_TONE_2_PRESENT = 0x004,
75  /*! Signalling tone 2 has changed state (ignored when setting tx mode) */
76  SIG_TONE_2_CHANGE = 0x008,
77  /*! The media signal is passing through. Tones might be added to it. */
78  SIG_TONE_TX_PASSTHROUGH = 0x010,
79  /*! The media signal is passing through. Tones might be extracted from it, if detected. */
80  SIG_TONE_RX_PASSTHROUGH = 0x040,
81  /*! Force filtering of the signalling tone, whether signalling is being detected or not.
82  This is mostly useful for test purposes. */
83  SIG_TONE_RX_FILTER_TONE = 0x080,
84  /*! Request an update of the transmit status, upon timeout of the previous status. */
85  SIG_TONE_TX_UPDATE_REQUEST = 0x100,
86  /*! Request an update of the receiver status, upon timeout of the previous status. */
87  SIG_TONE_RX_UPDATE_REQUEST = 0x200
